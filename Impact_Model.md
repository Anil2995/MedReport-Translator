# Business Impact Model: MedReport Translator

## Problem Addressed:
* 90%+ of patients do not understand their lab reports.
* Hospitals are overwhelmed with "clarification calls" post-discharge or post-test.
* Physicians spend 2+ hours a day re-explaining standardized data due to "white-coat hypertension."

## Quantified Business Impact (Year 1 Projection per 1,000 Patients)

### 1. Time Saved for Medical Professionals
* **Assumption:** Average "clarification call" to a clinic takes 8 minutes of a nurse/doctor's time. 
* **Frequency:** 40% of patients call their provider for help understanding their bloodwork before their follow-up appointment.
* **Reduction:** By providing the MedReport translation immediately alongside the lab results, we estimate reducing these calls by **60%**.
* **Math:** (400 calls * 60% reduction = 240 avoided calls) * 8 mins = **32 hours saved per month per average clinic.**

### 2. Operational Cost Reduction
* **Assumption:** The blended cost of triage nursing staff and administrative overhead is roughly $45/hour.
* **Math:** 32 hours saved * $45/hour = **$1,440 saved per clinic, per month.** For a mid-sized local hospital network (30 clinics), this scales to **$518,400 annually** just in reclaimed administrative and triage bandwidth.

### 3. Patient Experience & Revenue Recovery (Retention)
* **Problem:** Medical confusion causes patients to skip follow-up care ("health illiteracy attrition").
* **Solution:** By prompting the patient with "Questions to Ask Your Doctor," patients immediately see the value of their next appointment.
* **Math:** If a clinic sees a 5% increase in follow-up booking compliance (e.g., instead of missing the appointment for high glucose, the patient understands the severity and attends), this recovers **~$12,500** in lost consultation fees per 1,000 patients tested.

## The Bottom Line
MedReport Translator isn't just a patient convenience tool. It is an **Enterprise Cost Intelligence & Resource Optimization Engine**. It shifts the burden of base-level education from expensive clinical staff to scalable AI, while maintaining a perfectly audited, non-diagnostic safety guardrail.
